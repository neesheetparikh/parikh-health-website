If you've ever felt rushed through a 10-minute appointment, waited weeks for a same-day visit, or left your doctor's office feeling like you barely got to ask your real questions — you've experienced the limitations of the modern primary care model.

Concierge medicine was designed to fix exactly that.

## What Is Concierge Medicine?

Concierge medicine (also called direct primary care or membership medicine) is a practice model where patients pay a monthly or annual membership fee in exchange for a fundamentally different care experience.

Instead of a physician seeing 25–30 patients a day, a concierge physician limits their panel to a small group — typically 200–400 patients versus the standard 1,500–2,500. The result: more time, more access, and a deeper doctor-patient relationship.

## What You Actually Get

At ParikhHealth, our concierge members receive:

- **Same-day or next-day appointments** — always
- **Extended visits** — 30–60 minutes, not 10
- **Direct access to Dr. Parikh** via phone or text (for urgent matters)
- **Annual comprehensive physical** with thorough review of all systems
- **Proactive care** — we reach out to you, not the other way around
- **Care coordination** — we manage your specialist referrals and follow up on results personally

## What It Is Not

Concierge medicine is not:
- A replacement for health insurance (you still need insurance for specialists, hospitals, labs, imaging)
- Available only to the ultra-wealthy — our membership fees are straightforward and predictable
- A VIP-only club — we simply practice medicine the way it was always meant to be practiced

## Who Benefits Most?

Concierge medicine tends to be an excellent fit if you:

- Have complex or multiple chronic conditions that require close monitoring
- Frequently struggle to get timely appointments
- Want a physician who knows your complete health history — not just your last visit
- Travel frequently and want to be able to call your doctor from anywhere
- Are simply tired of feeling like a number

## Is the Cost Worth It?

This is a fair question. The value equation differs for everyone. Consider: how many hours have you spent waiting in urgent care for issues your primary doctor could have handled with a same-day visit? How much is peace of mind worth when a health concern arises at 8 PM?

For many patients, the answer is clear.
